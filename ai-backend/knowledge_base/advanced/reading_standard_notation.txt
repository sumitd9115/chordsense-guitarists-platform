LEVEL: Advanced
LESSON: Reading Standard Notation
============================================================

PAGE 1: Introduction to Standard Notation
----------------------------------------
Standard notation (sheet music) is the universal language of music — readable by a pianist, violinist, or guitarist anywhere in the world. Reading it unlocks a vastly larger world of musical resources.

  THE STAFF  
Standard notation uses a staff: 5 horizontal lines with notes placed on lines and in spaces. For guitar, the treble clef is used (the swirling symbol at the start of each staff).

  NOTE POSITIONS ON THE TREBLE CLEF  
Notes on lines (bottom to top): E - G - B - D - F ("Every Good Boy Does Fine")
Notes in spaces (bottom to top): F - A - C - E ("FACE")

  RHYTHM VALUES  
   Whole note:  4 beats (open oval, no stem)
   Half note:  2 beats (open oval with stem)
   Quarter note:  1 beat (filled oval with stem)
   Eighth note:  1/2 beat (filled oval with stem + flag or beam)
   Sixteenth note:  1/4 beat (filled oval with stem + double flag or beam)

  GUITAR IS A TRANSPOSING INSTRUMENT  
Guitar music is written one octave higher than it actually sounds. This is purely conventional — it keeps most guitar notes on or near the staff rather than requiring many ledger lines below it.

  WHY LEARN THIS?  
Access to the entire classical guitar repertoire, jazz lead sheets, and collaborative notation with non-guitar musicians. Even slow reading opens up a world of musical resources unavailable through tab alone.

PAGE 2: Key Signatures and Time Signatures
----------------------------------------
Key signatures and time signatures are the two fundamental pieces of information at the start of every piece of written music. Reading them correctly is the entry point to sight-reading.

  KEY SIGNATURES  
Placed immediately after the clef, a key signature shows which notes are consistently sharped or flatted throughout the piece.

  SHARP KEY SIGNATURES (sharps added in this order: F# C# G# D# A# E# B#)  
  No sharps = C major / A minor
  1 sharp (F#) = G major / E minor
  2 sharps (F# C#) = D major / B minor
  3 sharps (F# C# G#) = A major / F# minor
  4 sharps = E major / C# minor

  FLAT KEY SIGNATURES (flats added: Bb Eb Ab Db Gb Cb Fb)  
  1 flat (Bb) = F major / D minor
  2 flats (Bb Eb) = Bb major / G minor
  3 flats = Eb major / C minor
  4 flats = Ab major / F minor

  NATURAL SIGN  
A natural sign (♮) cancels the key signature for one specific note in that bar. After the bar line, the key signature applies again.

  TIME SIGNATURES  
The fraction after the key signature:
   Top number  = beats per bar
   Bottom number  = what note value = 1 beat (4 = quarter note, 8 = eighth note)

Common signatures:
   4/4:  4 quarter-note beats per bar (most common — "common time")
   3/4:  3 quarter-note beats per bar (waltz)
   6/8:  6 eighth-note beats per bar (compound duple — counted in 2 groups of 3)
   2/4:  2 quarter-note beats per bar (march)
   5/4:  5 quarter-note beats per bar (Dave Brubeck, Pink Floyd's "Money")
